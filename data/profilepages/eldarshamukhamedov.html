Summary

I grew up around computers. I started with C when I was ten, when my dad ran out of ways to deflect my "teach me how to make games" pleas. I led the software team of my high school's robotics club in the 2006 FIRST competition. I spent my university years working on small web design projects for local businesses, while messing around with Rome: Total War mods.

I started working for IGN in 2013, after graduating from their Code Foo internship program. Those six-weeks can only be described as a trial by fire, but experience was invigorating. In my time since, I've had the privilege of working with, and learning from, some very talented and passionate folks and friends.

While my strengths lie in front-end development, my career interests are constantly in motion. I cannot resists opportunities to work with technologies I am not familiar with. Right now, I am intrigued by backend JavaScript solutions and stack architecture design in general. Books lying open on my table deal with Node.js, MongoDB scaling, and reactive programming concepts.

In my free time, I enjoy story-driven games and good beer, Coltrane and fantasy novels, rainy weather (it's a Vancouver thing) and the Daily Show.
