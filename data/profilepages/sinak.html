Summary

I'm CEO of RSRF, a systems integrator specializing in small cells and distributed antenna systems targeting mid-size enterprises. We're quickly growing a nationwide team that's focussed on installing in-building cellular solutions. And we're hiring - get in touch!

I'm also a Technology Fellow at the Electronic Frontier Foundation, where I spent most of 2013/2014 couple of years building open democracy tools like democracy.io. I'm also co-founder and advisor to OpenSignal.com.

Between 2013 and mid-2015 I took a break from startups to work on political advocacy around technology issues. During that period:
- I started a petition to make cell phone unlocking legal, convinced the White House to support it, and lobbied Congress to pass a law to that effect. President Obama signed the "Unlocking Consumer Choice and Wireless Competition Act"​ on August 1st, 2014.
- I worked as a consultant for the Electronic Frontier Foundation, building open source tools to help people contact their members of Congress and on numerous campaigns around privacy and intellectual property.

I occasionally make small investments in startups, and am an advisor to three companies.

Experience

    RSRF

    RepeaterStore is a leading distributor and installer of passive distributed antenna systems. Our customers include NASA, Tesla, SpaceX, Stanford Unviersity, and many others. The company is based in Laguna Hills, CA.

  • Tech Fellow

    Electronic Frontier Foundation
    March 2014 – Present (2 years 10 months)San Francisco Bay Area

    I spent most of 2014 helping EFF build an open source toolset for online activism. Those tools are hosted at act.eff.org and include:

    - A full content management system for quickly creating actions.
    - A Twilio-integrated call tool that allows users to call US congress or any other political target.
    - An AWS-hosted, highly-scalable tool for sending hundreds of thousands of emails via congressional members contact forms.
    - The ability to quickly generate a custom tool targeting an email address or regulatory contact form.
    - Mobile apps that notify users when EFF needs their help, an analytics system, a partner-referral system, and much more.

    While I'm no longer full-time at EFF, I'm continuing on as a tech fellow to help coordinate new projects and help with specific campaigns.

  • Co-Founder and Advisor

    OpenSignal.com
    January 2010 – Present (7 years)London, United Kingdom

    OpenSignal is the world's largest crowdsourced mobile sensor network. With over 7 million downloads, 1 million active users and billions of unique data samples gathered per day, the OpenSignal application provides wireless market insights to major customers such as McKinsey, Telefonica and ChinaMobile. At the same time, the OpenSignal app uses the same database to help users find better signal in realtime, locate free WiFi hotspots and get personalised recommendations on the best mobile network for them.

    OpenSignal's second application, WeatherSignal, repurposes the pressure, temperature and humidity sensors on smartphones to collect data to provide better weather prediction to over 50k users, and has lead to a scientific publications in collaboration with the Royal Meteorlogical Society of the Netherlands demonstrating the potential of crowdsourced data for improving short term weather forecasts.

    We're pioneering the field of crowdsourced sensor networks and looking for smart, determined people to help us do it. If you'd like to join us check out our list of open positions or just get in touch: http://opensignal.com/jobs
