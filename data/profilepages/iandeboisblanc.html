  • Atmospheric Hexacopter Design Project

    July 2013 – July 2014

    Designed a hexacopter platform to repeatedly take atmospheric measurements in a vertical airspace profile. The platform ran on a linux based chip (BeagleBone Black) with an Arduino based flight controller using open sourced flight software. We tested and programmed multiple sensor packages designed to take temperature, pressure, humidity, and windspeed on the moving copter platform. In addition we tested the copters performance to maximize flight time, and programmed flight patterns to ensure data collection was not affected by flight speeds or irregularities. The purpose of this project was to develop a long term replacement for weather balloons that was reusable and programmable to fly at the flip of a switch. We won Best Design Project at the IEEE SIEDS 2014 conference held at UVA.

    Team members:
    • Ian deBoisblanc,
    • Rahul Mukherji,
    • Nikita Dodbele,
    • Max Newman,
    • Lee Kussman

  • JefferSat Cosmic Ray Cube Satellite

    Starting 2014

    In a cooperative mission with NASA, JefferSat is an Android-powered cube satellite carrying radiation sensors to measure the amount of cosmic radiation in Earth's upper atmosphere. As the Power, Thermal, and Environment team lead, I led research into upper atmospheric conditions to inform insulation design, and ensured every system on the satellite was properly powered and protected from cosmic damage.

    Team members:
    • Ian deBoisblanc
