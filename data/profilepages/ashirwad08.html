Projects

  • Next Word Predictor: An N-Gram Model Based Text Predictor App

    Starting March 2016

    Word! is a next word predictor, similar to the predictions of next typed word on your mobile phone keyboards. It's an n-gram driven model with a backoff implementation that computes the chained probabilities of quad-grams and tri-grams. Future enhancements include implemented Kneser-Ney smoothing and other point-of-speech approaches.

    The training was performed by a Swiftkey guided dataset from HC Corpora. This is a web crawler for blogs, twitter and news data.

    App is slow to load, give it a while. Code here: https://github.com/ashirwad08/typed-word-predictor.

    Tools: R, R Shiny, packages: Quanteda and Data.table

    Team members:
    • Ashirwad Chakraborty

  • DrivenData.org Project: "Warmup: Predict Blood Donations"

    Starting January 2016

    Drivendata.org is a 'Kaggle for social good' data hackathon site. In the top 20% as of Jan 07, 2016 with a LogLoss score of 0.4655.

    Link is a web application that can be used to predict based on some algorithms trained on the entire dataset. Corresponding presentation: http://rpubs.com/ashirwad/PredictBloodDonations

    This competition http://www.drivendata.org/competitions/2/ provides a "recency, frequency, monetize" styled dataset to try and predict blood donations in Taiwan for the month of March 07.

    After some feature engineering which includes creating a couple of new features, scaling variables, and analyzing multicollinearity, I've landed on Logistic Regression and Gradient Boosted Models as the best performing ones. The evaluation metric is the LogLoss function. Next steps are to try and improve the models by tuning the cost and regularization parameters. This is ongoing.

    Team members:
    • Ashirwad Chakraborty

  • Human Activity Recognition (HAR): Exercise Accuracy Predictor

    December 2015 – December 2015

    Predict Weight Lifting exercise classification by exploring the "Weight Lifting Exercises" dataset at http://groupware.les.inf.puc-rio.br/har. Engineer features by imputing missing values, removing superfluous features, analyzing near zero variance, density plots; evaluate the cross validation driven in sample and out of sample errors to pick the best performing classification algorithm. The Random Forests implementation wins out with a superior out of sample error rate (and cross validation accuracy) when compared to Recursive Partitioning Trees and Support Vector Machines.

    Team members:
    • Ashirwad Chakraborty
