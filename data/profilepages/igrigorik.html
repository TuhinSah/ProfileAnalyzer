Summary

Ilya Grigorik is a web performance engineer at Google, co-chair of the W3C Web Performance Working group, and author of High Performance Browser Networking (O'Reilly) book. In short, an internet plumber.

Experience

  • Co-chair of the W3C Web Performance working group

    W3C
    May 2015 – Present (1 year 8 months)

    The mission of the Web Performance Working Group is to develop browser APIs to help developers observe and improve aspects of web application performance.

  • Web Performance Engineer

    Google
    May 2012 – Present (4 years 8 months)

    Working on making the internet fast(er) both at Google and beyond: developing browser standards and API's; helping build better performance monitoring and developer infrastructure; writing, speaking, and teaching web performance best practices.

  • Software Engineering Manager, Google Analytics

    Google
    June 2011 – June 2012 (1 year 1 month)

    Led the integration of the PostRank team and the development and product launch of social analytics reporting within Google Analytics.

  • Founder & CTO

    PostRank
    January 2007 – June 2011 (4 years 6 months)

    Founded PostRank in early 2007 and led the product and engineering development teams. PostRank was acquired by Google in June 2011 and joined the Google Analytics group.

    PostRank tracks where and how users engage, and what they pay attention to - in real-time. PostRank social engagement data measures actual user activity, the most accurate indicator of the relevance and influence of a site, story, or author.

  • Founder

    ForteHost
    January 2002 – July 2007 (5 years 7 months)

    Founded and managed a web-hosting and security consulting company. Scaled operations to 250+ recurring clients with no additional employees.
