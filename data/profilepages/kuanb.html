Summary

I'm a researcher engineering digital tools with urban data; currently a in the Bay Area at Code for America. Prior, I was in New York City as a Civic Tech Fellow at Microsoft, where I developed performance data tools on top of MTA's One-Bus-Away implementation. Before that, I worked on scenario modeling software with the MIT Media Lab and Flocktracker - a transit network mapping tool - with the Singapore-MIT Alliance for Research and Technology while performing graduate studies at MIT.

  • Intermodal Transit Station (CETRAM) Research, Project Lead

    Grupo Prodi/Urban Travel Logistics

Lead the development of a research proposal jointly initated by Prof. Christopher Zegras of MIT and Florencia Serrania of UTL, managing a team of 2 UNAM students, 1 CS staff member, and 3 additional MIT MCP students. The research focused on the measuring comparative safety levels and their environmental externalities along routes operating from two reformed CETRAM stations in Mexico City, Mexico. Research focused on formalized Peseros and VW routes operating from these stations into the State of Mexico and sought to determine effects of comparative formal transit monitoring between these two stations and within their greater environments.
