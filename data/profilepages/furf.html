Summary

I have two decades of experience leading engineering, design and product teams to architect, build and ship everything from intuitive user interfaces to scalable system architectures for Major League Baseball, Mariah, Madonna, Meebo and myriad failed startups. Throughout my career, I have held a variety of senior leadership roles, including Director of Engineering at Huge, Technical Architect at MLB Advanced Media and Director of Product Development at Live Nation.

Most recently, I worked at Huge, a digital agency, where I architected and managed technical teams through the development of several projects for Google including Google's Material Design spec site, Think with Google's Customer Journey to Online Purchase tool, Google Express'​ Holiday Hacks promotion and Google's SMB portal, as well as two corporate intranets for ExxonMobil and a redesign of Logitech.com.

I am also an avid developer advocate and creative coder who presents at technology conferences internationally. I founded Huge's Developer Relations team and I've presented talks about JavaScript, digital art and open-source initiatives at JSConf, JSConf Brazil and jQuery Conference.

In 2015, I attended the UCSC Hackathon where I mentored and taught a second-year Computer Science major (with no prior web development experience) enough HTML, CSS and JavaScript to write an educational game about cellular biology that won the hackathon's top prize.
