Summary

I am a professor at UC Davis in the department of Statistics.
I am also the Director for the Data Science Initiative at UC Davis.

My research is in Statistical Computing, specifically infrastructure, computational paradigms and data technologies. I focus on hard questions that enable us to do new things in new ways.
I am involved in the development of R and about 100 R packages.
I have been involved in the development of S (specifically S4) at Bell Labs, the Omegahat environment, GGobi (a dynamic, interactive visualization system for multi-dimensional data) and a member of the NIMBLE development team.

I work on creating new tools to allow us all to do new things.

My current research focus is primarily on compilation tools within R, specifically using LLVM.

I am also very interested in pedagogy and specifically modernizing the statistics curriculum and data sciences teaching to incorporate both computing and authentic practice of data analysis.

I co-authored the book "XML and Web Technologies for Data Sciences with R"​ with Deb Nolan (Berkeley), and also the book "Data Science in R: A Case Studies Approach to Computational Reasoning and Problem Solving".
