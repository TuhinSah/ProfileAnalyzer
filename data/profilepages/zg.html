Summary

I am currently working full-time as a Platform Engineer for Cisco Spark.

Before transferring to the Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT), I was attending community college and working part-time as a web developer for a few small companies in my hometown. At WildFireWeb, I helped to design, build, manage and support websites of any kind including WordPress, Magento, Joomla, Drupal, WooCommerce, Bog Commerce as well as traditional HTML websites and those developed using 3rd party software and content management systems.

While studying Computer Science at RIT, I built a strong understanding of computing fundamentals, data structures, and algorithms - things that I was not exposed to before. This helped to foster a rich skill set and practical experience enabling me to become a valuable and creative contributor immediately upon graduation.

I have worked on many side projects in the last few years. Notable examples include writing a social network frontend, imageboard software, and a social network service.

My most recent project is an open source, community driven social network service that allows users to connect and interact with one another. The project is still in the very early stage of development, but I have already begun writing a backend service (in Go).

I run my own FreeBSD server with DigitalOcean, which houses my email, calendar, and contact servers, all of which I configured and got working for myself. I no longer rely on common services such as Google Apps or Apple iCloud because I setup my own solutions that I can manually tune to my specific needs.
