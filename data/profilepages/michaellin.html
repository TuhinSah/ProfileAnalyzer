Experience

  • Research Assistant

    Stanford University
    May 2014 – August 2014 (4 months)

    Principal Investigator: Allison Okamura, PhD
    Designed the OmniGrip, an open-source hand-held gripper device that attaches to the Phantom Omni to provide a 7-degrees-of-freedom haptic device for teleoperation of robotic manipulators. The user grips the OmniGrip at two actuated rotational levers, which renders programmed grip stiffness.

    • Currently working on a manuscript titled "The Effect of Manipulator Gripper Stiffness On
    Teleoperated Task Performance" for submission to 2015 IEEE Haptics Symposium.

    • Designed and built a circuit board that interfaces an Arduino microcontroller and serves as a
    motor controller to drive a motor for grip stiffness control.

    • Gained programming experience in ROS while working with the Raven II surgical system.

    • Conducting IRB-approved subject study with teammates to analyze the effect of master manipulator grip stiffness on a surgeon's performance.

    • Worked with 8051 microcontroller to interface the inertial sensor (Invensense MPU9150).

    • Explored various methods of sensor calibration and drift rejection such as Kalman filters.

Projects

  • MindScorpion RC Cars (H@B-IEEE Hackathon)

    Starting November 2012

    Two remote control (RC) cars were enhanced as our hardware hack project for the H@B-IEEE Hackathon of November 2012. The first one used the NeuroSky MindSet, an electroencephalogram (EEG), to detect brain waves and used variations in these brain waves to control the movement of the car. The second one was mounted with a Wii controller and a ultrasonic sensor to detect nearby 'targets'. Once near the target, the car would throw out its scorpion-like tail to sting the target with camera capacitors charged with hundreds of volts. Our project was awarded with "Best Hardware Hack" at the hackathon. See my website for more info.
