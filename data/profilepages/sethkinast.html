Summary

My programming passion revolves around the user interface. The most rewarding and successful projects are those that customers enjoy using, and part of delivering that success is creating beautiful, functional, and intuitive UI. This means being willing to challenge assumptions and not being satisfied with a project just because it works well-enough.

If I had to pick a motto to program by, it would be "Less is more." It matters little how many features and buzzwords you can cram into an app if it's not performant and easy-to-use. I'm looking for positions that will allow me to champion these values at a company that shares them.

I have experience with people management and I think my reports would tell you that I am an effective manager, able to keep a view of the big picture while still understanding their individual projects and career goals. However, I go to work every day to code, to create, so I'm not looking for a full-time manager role. Instead, I'd like to focus on technical leadership, in a role where I can help drive the overall vision and architecture of the product, and in a capacity that allows me to get my hands into code every day. Specifically, I'm interested in roles such as staff engineer or principal engineer, working full-time in a frontend-focused role.

While I do have full-stack experience across a number of languages and frameworks, I am happiest writing Javascript either on the frontend or in Node. I have recently become fascinated with React and its approach to views-- I feel like it's changed my approach to how I think about application design the most drastically of anything since Node and async programming. It's been my framework of choice for my recent side projects and I am definitely interested in hearing how you've been using it or similar frameworks like Vue.

Preferred languages / frameworks / tech: Javascript, HTML5, CSS, Node.js, React, Redux, Webpack, Git, Mocha
