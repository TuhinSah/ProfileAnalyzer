2. What about with equity? Yes, that is with equity.
3. We have a ping pong table. Cool. My base rate is still 160k.
4. Am I only looking in San Francisco? Yes, unless they are willing to pay significantly over my base rate. As in at lest 20k more.
5. Are you looking for Front end, back end or full stack? I prefer and am focused on the frontend. I can and will do some work on the back end; most especially, if that back end is Node.js.
7. What about React.js? I have done playing around with it and I like it; am open to React.js, but I can't say I have hand's on with it.
8. Are you open to [Java, Scala, Python, Dart, A Language We Invented] I'm generally open to any language -- but its a lot easier to sell me in the areas I am proven.

Size in my experience is not an absolute determinant of job satisfaction; however, if your company is so big that a lot of developers mill around and do busy work while the managers balance their agendas, I won't be around very long. So its a matter of, is your company efficient and responsive? Too small can be inefficient through a paucity of specialists. So I am looking for a company that is big enough to do the job.
