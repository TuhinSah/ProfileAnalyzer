  • Legal Ads Policy Domain Specialist

    Twitter
    October 2009 – March 2014 (4 years 6 months)

    Wrote Twitter's initial set of abuse policies around many aspects of site use relating to intellectual property, free speech, and abuse. As an early member of a small team, I was responsible for developing processes and workflows from scratch in order to uphold user trust in Twitter. I made and defended decisions about content removal that received national media attention.

    In 2010, when Twitter launched its first paid advertising products, I had the responsibility of working with Revenue Product Management and Sales to define Twitter Ads policies. This included advertiser removal rules, international ad content guidelines, and spam prevention. I became an expert in illegal content, consumer protection, and digital advertising.

    As the team grew from one person to seven, I transitioned to a domain specialist role supporting Twitter's syndicated ad products. I helped on policy integration when Twitter acquired the MoPub ad exchange, and represented Twitter at an FTC conference on native advertising. I was particularly drawn toward communication issues related to policy.

    I started an initiative to make policy-setting a highly cross-functional effort; I created and led a working group, split between Sales, Ops, and Policy, designed to gather Sales input and metrics before and after any policy change. The goal was to ensure that Twitter's ads policies made sense to the marketplace. This group, called "Pelicans", still exists at the company.

    The largest project I managed is a resource with all of Twitter's policies for advertisers, twitter.com/adspolicy. In addition to being a complete content resource, it's linked in all of Twitter's anti-abuse systems, translated into seven languages, and featured within Twitter Ads marketing collateral. It has dramatically reduced the number of ad hoc questions the team receives.
