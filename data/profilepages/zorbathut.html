Summary

I like making things. Specifically, I like making games. Code's a tool used to make things happen, and it's a tool I've gotten quite good at.

I'm expert at tackling existing codebases, fixing them up so they run faster and with fewer bugs, and bending them to fill new requirements. This is the game industry; we all know games change many times between spec sheet and release. I can make that happen!

As of this writing I'm expert at rendering and UI, experienced in gameplay, networking, tools, AI, and everything else game-related, and working to get more experience in those as well. I can work with Unity, Unreal Engine 4, and custom engines, and I'm great at ramping up on new tools and new projects.

I want to make your games. Talk to me.

Experience

  • Lead Programmer

    Crimson Keep
    September 2015 – Present (1 year 4 months)

    Sole programmer on two-person indie game, currently in early development. After several iterations on character movement and the ability system (it's fun now) we're fleshing it out into a vertical slice demo. From there, hopefully a full-size game. Unity, first-person roguelike.

  • Senior Rendering Engineer

    Trion Worlds
    October 2010 – Present (6 years 3 months)

    Rift: Unexpectedly became Rift's lead rendering engineer immediately before major product launch. Developed and polished features in time for release. Continued work afterwards, including major client optimizations; so far, have roughly doubled framerate while preserving graphic quality, adding new features, and maintaining stability.

    Lead developer and architect for the Addon system, significant UI engineering work. Involved in launch, two major expansions, approximately two dozen minor updates, and dozens of patches.

    Defiance: Short stint on the Defiance team to help with a major patch. Redesigned a major game component within a very constrained time schedule to match already-announced release date and marketing.

    Atlas Reactor: Implemented replay system and reconnection system within an existing codebase, refactoring several legacy systems in the process. Took over empty audio engineering role while providing rendering backup. Involved in launch, remaining on team to the present day.

  • Curator

    Digital Game Museum
    2009 – Present (7 years)

    Co-founder, boardmember, curator for non-profit Digital Game Museum. Lead exhibit designer for over a dozen exhibits. Lead visual designer for handouts, brochures, T-shirts, and the like.

  • Director

    Mandible Games
    September 2006 – January 2013 (6 years 5 months)

    Sole developer on indie studio. Developed over a dozen experimental games. Currently somewhat on hiatus.

    Between June 2009 - October 2010, one game per month, each at most one week. Exploratory process to figure out how to write a fun game. All games available at www.mandible.net. Recommended games linked on sidebar.

    Ludum Dare 17: “Nieuwe Aarde” placed first in Theme, placed 11th overall.

  • Lead Developer

    QuestHelper
    July 2008 – January 2010 (1 year 7 months)

    Took over, maintained, and rewrote QuestHelper, at the time the world’s most popular World of Warcraft addon with estimated userbase between 1 and 2 million. Learned Lua in the process. Self-sustaining off donations until Blizzard implemented their own QuestHelper clone within the game.

  • Engineer

    Google

  • Engineer
