Summary

Software engineer with a strong UNIXy/Linux background. Since I worked at Google, I specialize in writing & running large-scale distributed serving systems that serve millions of users. I have a deep understanding of the entire technology stack (including Google's), from on-wire protocols and low-level implementation details all the way up to high-level designs used in high-availability distributed systems (both in software and in the datacenter). I'm always eager to learn new things and also enjoy teaching what I know.

Although I've been part of operations teams of some sort for a while, in my heart I'm a true software engineer. I love tackling challenging problems (especially if they've never been solved before) and automate myself out of job. I'm very interested in research & development and frequently read scientific articles from various conferences.

I have a scientific, pragmatic & data-driven approach to things. That's why, for example, I wrote OpenTSDB, the first open-source monitoring system that was truly scalable and distributed. I've used it with a great deal of success to understand complex / chaotic infrastructures, pinpoint the root cause of their problems, and improve them iteratively.

I love to take a deep dive into the guts of the systems I run, read their code, understand their algorithms and data structures. I've spent a great deal of time reading and learning from the source code of the libstdc++, libc, Linux kernel, OpenJDK, Go, and most libraries and tools I discover and use. For this reason, I also prefer to avoid all closed-source and proprietary systems as much as possible.

Specialties: Strong expertise in distributed serving systems design and operations, for user-facing services used by millions of users.
I master virtually all mainstream programming languages, especially C/C++ & Java/Scala.
I can learn *any* new technology or programming language in a matter of days, and become an expert in it in a matter of weeks (provided that it's interesting :D).

  • Site Reliability Engineer, Tech Lead

    StumbleUpon
    December 2009 – July 2012 (2 years 8 months)

    Responsible of StumbleUpon's entire infrastructure, tech lead of the operations/infrastructure team (8 ppl), directly responsible for a yearly budget >$1M. Led the company through a >3x traffic growth period.

    Highlights of my contributions.
    - Created a new monitoring system, OpenTSDB, the only (to date) open-source, highly scalable, distributed monitoring system. OpenTSDB has the largest (by item count) and fastest growing data set at StumbleUpon, with ~500B data points.
    - Wrote an asynchronous, non-blocking, thread-safe, high-performance HBase client that is used to serve over 70% of our traffic, and used by OpenTSDB to store >1 billion data points per day. This client scales and performs better than HBase's own client.
    - Wrote a poor man's system call tracing tool to profile backend interactions and extract granular information on how our applications behave and troubleshoot them when there are backend issues. This tool has been amazingly helpful to quickly get down to the root cause of many outages.
    - Rewrote an internal pub-sub type system handling north of 10K messages/s with 99.999% uptime. The previous (legacy) implementation couldn't keep up as traffic increased.
    - Wrote a badge server in Scala, capable of serving 60 to 75% of all of StumbleUpon's HTTP requests out of a single machine with a mean latency of less than 1ms. N+2 setup with a 99th percentile below 10ms and 99.995% uptime.
    - Wrote a framework that uses MapReduce to extract large HBase tables (~40B key-values), and enable jobs to mmap() the data, to do exhaustive analysis in about 90sec vs hours with Hive.
    - Gave a lot of advices to the rest of the eng organization on distributed systems design, using various technologies, building scalable services, performance tuning and debugging pretty much anything broken.
    - Completely rebuilt datacenters, did network design.
    - Redid load balancing infrastructure.
    - Chose hardware.
    - Hired several engineers. Conducted >100 interviews.
