  • CaMor: Generating Interpolating 3D Animation Using Perspective Projections from a Single Image

    December 2014 – August 2015

    CaMor is a framework for generating a 3D animation from a single drawing or photograph that represents a perspective projection of a planar shape. The user identifies the 3 lines that contain these edge portions, for example by clicking on 4 or 6 points on the image, and indicates where on the screen these lines should be at the end of the animation. CaMor produces the animation by combining (1) a new rectification procedure that identifies the orientation of a rectangle from the image of a part of its perspective projection, (2) a depth adjustment step that ensures that the two rectified rectangles are congruent in 3D, (3) a screw motion interpolation in 3D, and (4) a perspective projection.

    Team members:
    • Gokul Raghuraman
