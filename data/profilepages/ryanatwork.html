Summary

I have a Masters in Public Administration (MPA) and Bachelors of Science in Computer Science from California State University Northridge (CSUN).

I was previosly a Senior Engineer at Upworthy where I did Ruby on Rails engineering. I joined Upworthy in January 2013 when the company was only 11 months old and was hired as the 4th engineer.

In 2012 I was an Engineer on the Tech Team at Obama for America. We helped re-elect the President of the United States. Prior to OFA I worked at Code for America as the Technical Lead. I was also an inaugural 2011 Code for America Fellow. Code for America is a non profit technology startup based in San Francisco that brings top web professionals to give a year of service building web 2.0 apps for local governments. At Code for America I have worked on many open source projects including creating Ruby Gems and ArcGIS applications.

Prior to Code for America I worked for the City of Santa Clarita as a Senior Information Technology Analyst in the division of Technology Services. I have over 5+ years of experience developing local government applications and management in the Information Technology sector of local government. I began working at the City of Santa Clarita as an Application Development Contractor and moved with increasing responsibility each year from IT Specialist, IT Analyst and Senior IT Analyst.

I have a wide variety of experience developing applications for local government. This includes land use management software, permitting software, ArcGIS mapping and various other local government applications. I am very active in the Government 2.0 space and follow all trends and practices

Specialties: Ruby on Rails, Open Source Software, Project Management, Esri ArcGIS, ArcGIS Flex API, ArcGIS Silverlight API, ArcGIS Javascript API, Social Media, HTML, CSS

Note to Recruiters (if you have read this far): I no longer do .NET please don't send a message about .NET I'm not interested in ever doing .NET again.
