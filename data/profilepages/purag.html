Summary

I'm a Computer Science major at the University of California, San Diego. My background is in full stack web development with HTML5, CSS3, Javascript (with jQuery), and PHP. I've been building websites for almost a decade, gaining extensive experience developing from scratch and for content management systems like WordPress. I also have thorough experience in C, C++, and Java.

I'm currently a tutor in the Computer Science & Engineering department at UC San Diego for an Introduction to Computer Science & Java course that focuses on object-oriented programming with Java. My responsibilities include conducting help hours in the computer science labs as well as grading student assignments and leading instructional sessions to go over upcoming programming assignments.

I have a passion for computational mathematics and plan on pursuing my education beyond my undergraduate studies. I'm also passionate about education and its intersections with technology; I originally chose computer science to explore these intersections and build software solutions for classrooms and schools as a whole.

Volunteer Experience & Causes

  • Participant, China Study Tour 2014

    L&Z Global Study Tours
    May 2014 – June 2014 (2 months)Education

    From May 20 to June 2, 2014, 12 students from Aveson Global Leadership Academy (including myself), through a partnership with L&Z Global Study Tours, visited Beijing, China. Housed at Beijing Changping New Oriental Foreign Language School (NOFLS), we directly experienced the accelerated and difficult Chinese education system. After a few short days touring Beijing, we worked with our Chinese student partners on a week of volunteer teaching at Nanqijia School, a primary and junior high school for children of migrant workers. At Nanqijia, we led classrooms of 50 students in lessons in American culture, and enjoyed outdoor activities and brief English language lessons.

    Global service is a central pillar of my educational philosophy. I was very eager to take part in a project that could embody the goals I worked for in my high school career and also set me on a path to further engaging in global service opportunities.
