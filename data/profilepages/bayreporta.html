Summary

John Osborn D'Agostino, aka Bayreporta, is an Oakland-based web developer, data journalist and game designer. He’s passionate about journalism games, data visualization and open government. His goal is to make games that incorporate the best of journalistic storytelling and investigation with the best of game design.

Projects

  • "Parachute Kids"​ Web Game

    Starting October 2016

    Unity-based web journalism game created from numerous interviews with current and past "parachute kids," young kids who come to the U.S. from abroad, often alone or without parental support, to attend American schools. The game, built in a narrative-driven choose your own adventure format puts the player in the shoes of one of these kids. You'll have to juggle wellbeing, language proficiency and GPA to succeed in your goal of being accepted into an American university.

    Team members:
    • John Osborn D'Agostino,
    • Tiffany Lew

  • Gimme Props!

    August 2012 – November 2012

    In an attempt to inform and engage younger audiences with state politics, I collaborated with six other Berkeley j-school students to create a light-hearted, interactive guide to each of the California propositions on the 2012 ballot.

    The project was a finalist for an Online News Association award in 2013.

    I was the project manager. I delegated tasks, illustrated most of the pixel art, coded the framework we used for the project, and helped with research.

    Team members:

    • Erik Reyna,

  • Shine in Peace

    January 2013 – January 2015

    Shine in Peace is an Oakland-based project launched by Susie Cagle which aims to humanize gun violence in Oakland. Using narrative and data, the project seeks to offer an in-depth look at the causes and consequences of gun violence at the human and policy level.

    My role in the project is focused primarily on data analysis and data visualization.

    Team members:
    • John Osborn D'Agostino,
    • Susie Cagle

  • Oakland Shootings 2011 Data App

    November 2012 – December 2012

    This project was published on Oakland North in December 2012. I used the Google Fusion API to build out a multilayered heat map showing shootings and victims by Oakland police beat. Also, when you click on a police beat, the two graphs under the map dynamic change to reflect the data of the geographic area picked.

    Team members:
    • John Osborn D'Agostino,
    • Mihir Zaveri

  • OpenDisclosure California

    Starting January 2015

    Open Disclosure California links campaign finance data from local cities and counties with state-level data to create a more comprehensive picture about who is influencing elections. We track contributions to candidates and ballot measures at the state and local level, as well as expenses.

    The project is California’s first online and open source repository combining local and state campaign finance data. The goal is to build an automated pipeline from existing data sources that feeds up-to-date state and local campaign finance data into our system. A free, open API will allow others to use this data for their own projects. A website we build and maintain will present the data on all races and ballot measures for the local jurisdictions that have their data loaded into our system, as well as statewide races.

    By feeding local and state data into our system, we can better connect the campaign finance dots and provide important context as to the big players influencing elections. The accompanying website will offer rich visualizations to better allow people to conceptualize campaign spending in their communities. Interested parties who wish to dig deeply into the actual data will be able to do so for their own projects.

    Team members:
    • John Osborn D'Agostino
