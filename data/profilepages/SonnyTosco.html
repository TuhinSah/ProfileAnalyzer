Summary

Hello! My name is Sonny Tosco. I'm a 2006 graduate of the United States Military Academy at West Point, father, husband, full-stack developer, and startup founder. I've gotten to this point in life by maintaining long-term vision in times of adversity, winning the perennial hardest worker award in every organization I'm in, and being unafraid to learn from every experience.

Upon graduating from West Point in 2006, I spent 6 years of my life on active duty as a Captain in the US Army. I have successfully demonstrated my ability to lead people and manage resources through three deployments where I served in pivotal roles to our mission's success. When I ended my career, I was rated by executive leaders in the "top 1% of all officers in a 13 year career", with "unlimited potential".

After I decided to transition out of the military, I was drawn to come back home to San Jose and start a career for myself in the tech industry. I had difficulty finding work because of my military experience so I decided to start my own tech company; Limelight. Limelight was a social app that allows users to ask people on a map to send them a photo of that location. This experience allowed me to lead a team through the iterative process of product development. Our team created a product that grew to 2,500 users 35% of which were daily actives with an acquisition cost of $1.10/user over a 5 month period.

The biggest lesson learned from that experience is the need to understand what a software developer does. I decided to attend Coding Dojo in July, and am now proficient in Python, Swift, and the MEAN Stack. Reading and writing code is a skillset that I feel will be ubiquitous in the tech industry.

When I'm not working, I love to spend time with my little family (my 3-year old son Bugatti and my 1-month old daughter Aimia) and also am advancing my skills in programming. Let's grab a coffee and talk tech, startups, the meaning of life, and how I can help.
