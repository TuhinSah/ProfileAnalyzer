Summary

I am a software engineer and entrepreneur. My personal mission is to help others through entrepreneurship and technology. Most of my work can be categorized into three categories: practice, education, and outreach.

As a software engineer, it is important to be an active practitioner. At Playpen Labs, a software consultancy that I founded, I am responsible for architecting and building software solutions for external clients and internal projects.

In addition to Playpen Labs, I work part-time at Hackbright Academy, a software engineering academy for women. At Hackbright Academy, I am responsible for developing curriculum and teaching the Front-End Development and Intro to Programming course.

My passions include "leveling the playing fields", as it applies to gender and race diversity in both the tech workforce and tech entrepreneurship. In 2011, I cofounded a nonprofit, Black Founders, whose mission is to increase the number of black entrepreneurs in tech. Currently, Black Founders, operates nationwide with mentoring programs, tech conferences, and hackathons at Historically Black College and Universities (HBCU).

I obtained my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from University of Maryland Eastern Shore, an hbcu. I also have a Master of Business Administration with a concentration in Management Information Systems from Drexel University.

I am available to consult on projects relating to software development, computer science curriculum development, and gender and race diversity training. Please contact me at me@hadiyah.me for both consultation and speaking opportunities.
