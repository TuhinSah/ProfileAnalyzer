Summary

I am a 20 year old computer enthusiast always eager to learn something new. I am mostly obsessed with kernel and driver development, but I also love Java, perl and C for userspace development as well. I'm currently engaged in learning Haskell, a purely functional language.

I hack the Linux kernel on a daily basis. I mostly do ATA, ACPI and drivercore fixes. I am obsessed with kobjects. They are beautiful. Right now I'm into two projects:
* Showing a QR code when the kernel crashes. The goal is to make error reporting even easier.
* an ivshmem driver. Originally intended for personal uses, now thinking about getting it upstream.

I am also a KDE developer, where I work on Marble (Virtual Globe) and KDevelop.
I got my KDE Developer account during the 2013 iteration of the open source competition called Google Code-In. I was chosen to be one of the five finalists of KDE.

I was an intern on the Virtualization team for Red Hat during the summers of 2014 and 2015. Amazing experience!

Publications

  • Linux Desktop: When is our Year?

    SCALE 13x in Los Angeles, CA
    February 2015

    We will start with some information about the current Desktop market, the status of the Linux Desktop and its different environments. First, we will list some problems I found and discuss each of them, one-by-one, while showing points where they are related to eachother. These problems vary in nature from community problems to diversity problems while including the problem of the enterprise overriding the needs of the desktop. While some of these problems are not that important, when there are multiples of them, they are problems. The community must work together to solve these problems and make sure that the people who aren't tech-savvy can use a Linux distribution and we must ensure that they enjoy it.
